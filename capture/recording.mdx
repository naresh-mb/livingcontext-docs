Recording is designed to disappear into the background: start a capture, do the work the way you normally would, and every meaningful action becomes a documented step.

## Start recording

Open the side panel and click **Start Capture**. From that moment, every click, text entry, and navigation becomes a step — each with a screenshot captured at the exact moment of the action, so the page looks the way it did when you acted on it.

Steps stream into the side panel in real time, so you can see the guide taking shape as you work.

## What becomes a step

| Action | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Click | One step, with the clicked element highlighted in the screenshot |
| Typing in a field | One step per field, showing what you entered |
| Navigation | One step marking the page change |
| Deliberate keys | One step each — <kbd>⌘</kbd>/<kbd>Ctrl</kbd> + <kbd>S</kbd>/<kbd>C</kbd>/<kbd>V</kbd>/<kbd>X</kbd>, <kbd>Enter</kbd>, <kbd>Escape</kbd> |

Typing is always aggregated into a single step per field — never one step per keystroke. Bare letters, arrow keys, and <kbd>Tab</kbd> are ignored entirely, so incidental keyboard activity never clutters the guide (and is never recorded — see [Privacy and redaction](/capture/privacy-and-redaction)).

## Pause and resume

Pause the capture at any time from the side panel — for example, to look something up or handle an interruption you don't want documented. While paused, nothing is recorded.

The paused state is durable: it survives page reloads and carries into new tabs, so a capture never silently re-arms itself mid-pause.

## Session video and typing GIFs

Alongside the step-by-step record, the extension can record a session video of the current browser tab:

- Video is **tab-only** — the extension cannot record your monitor or other windows.
- Recordings run up to **15 minutes** per capture.
- The video is published with the guide as a playable recording.

Typing steps also get short, automatically generated GIF clips at publish time, so readers can see exactly how a field was filled in.

## Complex pages just work

The extension records reliably inside modern enterprise apps:

- **Iframes** — steps and screenshots capture correctly even when the page renders inside a frame, as Salesforce Classic does.
- **Shadow DOM** — components in Salesforce Lightning and other web-component UIs are captured like any other element.

You don't need to do anything special — record the workflow as you'd normally perform it.

<Tip>
Keep captures focused on one task. Shorter, single-purpose guides are easier to follow, easier to replay with [Guide Me](/capture/guide-me), and stay well within the 60-step limit per capture.
</Tip>

## Finish

When you're done, stop the capture from the side panel. Your steps are saved locally in the browser and ready for [editing](/capture/editing-steps) — nothing leaves your machine until you [publish](/capture/publishing).
