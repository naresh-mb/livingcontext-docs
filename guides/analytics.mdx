**Analytics** turns your synced Salesforce records into charts and dashboards without leaving LivingContext. Everything renders natively in the app — in the same theme as the rest of your workspace, with no external tools, pop-outs, or public share links.

Open it from **Analytics** in the left navigation. Anyone who can read your docs can view analytics.

## What you can chart

Analytics reads the records behind your Salesforce org — Accounts, Opportunities, Cases, your custom objects — and renders aggregates and trends across the full chart catalog:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Trends and comparisons" icon="chart-line">
    Bar, row, line, area, and combo charts for breakdowns and change over time.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Distributions" icon="chart-pie">
    Pie, scatter and bubble, funnel, waterfall, and sankey charts.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Single numbers" icon="gauge">
    Scalar, trend (vs. the previous period), gauge, and progress indicators.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Detail" icon="table">
    Tables when you need the underlying rows.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Filter without leaving the page

Every chart is **dynamically filterable**. Filter controls come from the chart's own columns — category multi-selects, date ranges, and contains-search — and apply instantly to what's on screen. Dashboards get a single shared filter bar that spans every chart on the board.

<Note>
Filters narrow what you see on the page; they never change the underlying report or your Salesforce data.
</Note>

## Create charts by asking

You don't have to build charts by hand. Ask the [workspace agent](/agents/workspace-agent) on **Home** for a chart or dashboard in plain language — "chart new Opportunities by month this year", "build a dashboard of pipeline by stage and owner" — and it renders the result **inline in the chat**. Saved charts and dashboards also show up on the Analytics page, where you can open and filter them any time.

<Tip>
Deep-link a specific chart or dashboard by opening it from the Analytics list — the URL captures which one you're viewing so you can share it with teammates (they'll need access to sign in).
</Tip>
